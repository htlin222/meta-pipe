## Interpretation

### Key Findings

1. **PD-L1-Positive Patients (N=1212)**:
   - Pooled pCR RR 1.27 (95% CI 1.10–1.45, p=0.018)
   - Absolute pCR benefit: +14.1% (66.7% vs 52.6%)
   - NNT = 7 patients

2. **PD-L1-Negative Patients (N=284)**:
   - Pooled pCR RR 1.75 (95% CI 0.09–33.96, p=0.25)
   - Absolute pCR benefit: +15.3% (52.0% vs 36.7%)
   - NNT = 7 patients
   - Wide CI due to small sample size (k=2 trials) and heterogeneity (I²=29.9%)

3. **Interaction Test**: p=0.169 (not statistically significant)
   - No evidence of differential treatment effect by PD-L1 status
   - Point estimates suggest similar or greater benefit in PD-L1-negative patients

4. **KEYNOTE-522 EFS Data**: PD-L1-negative patients showed numerically greater benefit (HR 0.48 vs 0.67), directly contradicting the predictive biomarker hypothesis

### Clinical Implications

**PD-L1 is Prognostic, Not Predictive**:

- PD-L1-positive patients have higher baseline pCR rates (52.6% vs 36.7% in control arms), indicating PD-L1 is associated with better prognosis
- However, both PD-L1-positive and PD-L1-negative patients derive similar or greater benefit from ICI therapy (interaction p=0.169)
- The numerically greater EFS benefit in PD-L1-negative patients (KEYNOTE-522: HR 0.48 vs 0.67) suggests PD-L1 does not identify patients who differentially benefit from ICI

**Treatment Recommendations**:

- **DO NOT** use PD-L1 testing to exclude patients from neoadjuvant ICI therapy
- Both PD-L1-positive and PD-L1-negative TNBC patients should be offered ICI plus chemotherapy
- Current NCCN, ASCO, and ESMO guidelines do not require PD-L1 testing for neoadjuvant ICI in TNBC

**Contrast with Metastatic TNBC**:

- In metastatic TNBC (IMpassion130), benefit was restricted to PD-L1-positive patients (PD-L1 IC ≥1%)
- The discrepancy may reflect:
  - Enhanced immune priming potential in neoadjuvant setting with intact lymphatic drainage
  - Greater antigen presentation from chemotherapy-induced tumor cell death before surgery
  - Different biology of treatment-naïve vs heavily pretreated tumors

### Limitations

1. **Heterogeneous PD-L1 Definitions**:
   - CPS ≥1 (KEYNOTE-522, CamRelief): Combined Positive Score
   - IC ≥1% (IMpassion031, NeoTRIPaPDL1): Immune Cell percentage
   - Investigator-defined (GeparNuevo): Non-standardized
   - Limits direct comparability across trials

2. **Limited PD-L1-Negative Sample Size**:
   - Only 284 patients (12% of total cohort) across 2 trials
   - Wide confidence intervals reduce precision of effect estimate
   - Larger studies needed to confirm benefit in PD-L1-negative subgroup

3. **Missing Data**:
   - NeoTRIPaPDL1 and CamRelief did not report PD-L1 subgroup data
   - Only KEYNOTE-522 reported EFS and OS by PD-L1 status
   - Individual patient data meta-analysis would enable more robust subgroup assessment
