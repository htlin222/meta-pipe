# Supplementary Table 4. GRADE Evidence Profile

## Summary of Findings: ICI Plus Chemotherapy vs Chemotherapy Alone for Neoadjuvant Treatment of Triple-Negative Breast Cancer

**Population**: Patients with early-stage or locally advanced triple-negative breast cancer (stage II-III, ER <1-10%, PR <1-10%, HER2-negative)
**Intervention**: Immune checkpoint inhibitor (PD-1 or PD-L1 inhibitor) plus neoadjuvant chemotherapy
**Comparison**: Neoadjuvant chemotherapy alone (with or without placebo)
**Setting**: Neoadjuvant (pre-operative) treatment in randomized controlled trials

## GRADE Working Group Grades of Evidence

**High certainty (⊕⊕⊕⊕)**: We are very confident that the true effect lies close to that of the estimate of the effect.

**Moderate certainty (⊕⊕⊕◯)**: We are moderately confident in the effect estimate: the true effect is likely to be close to the estimate of the effect, but there is a possibility that it is substantially different.

**Low certainty (⊕⊕◯◯)**: Our confidence in the effect estimate is limited: the true effect may be substantially different from the estimate of the effect.

**Very low certainty (⊕◯◯◯)**: We have very little confidence in the effect estimate: the true effect is likely to be substantially different from the estimate of effect.

## Detailed GRADE Assessment by Outcome

### 1. Pathologic Complete Response (pCR)

| GRADE Domain                         | Rating                   | Justification                                                                                                                                             |
| ------------------------------------ | ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Study Limitations (Risk of Bias)** | No serious limitations   | All 5 trials at low or some concerns for bias. Four trials double-blind. Sensitivity analysis excluding NeoTRIPaPDL1 (open-label) showed similar results. |
| **Inconsistency**                    | No serious inconsistency | I² = 0%, Cochran's Q p=0.85. Remarkably consistent effect across different ICI agents, chemotherapy regimens, and patient populations.                    |
| **Indirectness**                     | No serious indirectness  | Direct comparison of ICI + chemo vs chemo alone in target population (stage II-III TNBC). pCR is FDA-validated surrogate endpoint.                        |
| **Imprecision**                      | No serious imprecision   | Large sample (N=2402), narrow 95% CI (1.16-1.37), highly significant (p=0.0015). Optimal information size exceeded.                                       |
| **Publication Bias**                 | Undetected               | Egger's test p=0.713. Comprehensive search including conference abstracts. All registered trials reported results.                                        |
| **Overall Certainty**                | ⊕⊕⊕⊕ **HIGH**            | No reasons to downgrade. Robust, consistent evidence of clinically meaningful benefit.                                                                    |

**Clinical Impact**: For every 7 patients treated with ICI + chemotherapy (vs chemotherapy alone), 1 additional patient achieves pCR. pCR is validated as surrogate for improved survival in TNBC.

| GRADE Domain                         | Rating                   | Justification                                                                                                                        |

| **Study Limitations (Risk of Bias)** | No serious limitations   | All 3 trials at low risk of bias. Double-blind, adequate randomization and allocation concealment.                                   |
| **Inconsistency**                    | No serious inconsistency | I² = 0%, consistent direction of effect across all 3 trials (HRs: 0.65, 0.76, 0.54).                                                 |
| **Indirectness**                     | No serious indirectness  | Direct measurement of recurrence, second cancer, or death in target population.                                                      |

**Clinical Impact**: For every 11 patients treated with ICI + chemotherapy, 1 additional patient remains alive and disease-free at 5 years.

## Overall Benefit-Risk Assessment

### Magnitude of Benefits

- **pCR**: HIGH certainty, NNT=7 (1 in 7 achieve additional pCR)
- **EFS**: MODERATE certainty, NNT=11 (1 in 11 avoid recurrence/death at 5 years)
- **OS**: LOW certainty, NNT=11 (1 in 11 additional alive at 5 years), both individual trials significant

### Magnitude of Harms

- **Serious AE**: LOW certainty, NNH=10 (1 in 10 experience serious AE, mostly reversible)
- **Grade 3+ irAE**: LOW certainty, NNH=9 (1 in 9 experience Grade 3+ irAE, most reversible)
- **Fatal AE**: LOW certainty, NNH=250 (1 in 250 experience fatal AE)

### Clinical Conclusion

The **benefit-risk profile strongly favors ICI plus chemotherapy**:

- Curative potential (NNT=7-11) substantially outweighs manageable toxicity (NNH=9-10)
- Fatal events are rare (NNH=250) and within acceptable range for cancer therapy
- pCR improvement translates to durable survival benefits (validating surrogate endpoint)
- Most adverse events are reversible with established management protocols

### Guideline Recommendations

- **NCCN Guidelines (2024)**: Category 1 recommendation for pembrolizumab + chemotherapy in stage II-III TNBC
- **ASCO Guidelines (2024)**: Strong recommendation for ICI + chemotherapy in neoadjuvant TNBC
- **ESMO Guidelines (2023)**: Recommended as standard of care for stage II-III TNBC
- **PD-L1 testing**: NOT required for treatment selection (both PD-L1+ and PD-L1- patients benefit)

## Footnotes

¹**Imprecision (EFS)**: Confidence interval spans both moderate (HR 0.86, ~6% absolute benefit) and large (HR 0.51, ~15% absolute benefit) clinical effects. Follow-up duration varies across trials (36-75 months), and late recurrences beyond 5 years may modify estimates.

²**Very serious imprecision (OS)**: Hartung-Knapp adjustment with k=2 trials (df=1) produces extremely wide confidence interval (0.00-128.74) as statistical penalty for small number of studies. This does not negate the highly significant individual trial results (both p<0.01) or the biological plausibility from concordance with pCR and EFS.

³**Inconsistency (OS)**: Statistical heterogeneity I²=62.3% reflects different magnitude of effect between KEYNOTE-522 (HR 0.66, 5-year benefit 4.9%) and GeparNuevo (HR 0.26, 5-year benefit 12.0%). Heterogeneity may be due to GeparNuevo's smaller sample (N=174), more stringent pCR definition (ypT0 vs ypT0/Tis), or chance.

⁴**Indirectness (Safety)**: Serious adverse event definitions varied between trials. Grade 3+ irAE data reported in detail by only 1 trial (KEYNOTE-522), limiting generalizability. However, real-world cohorts corroborate similar rates.

⁵**Imprecision (Safety)**: Limited number of trials (k=1-2) and moderate sample sizes for safety outcomes. Confidence intervals include both small and large harms. Real-world data provide external validation but not pooled precision.

⁶**Very serious imprecision (Fatal AE)**: Extremely rare events (4 deaths among 1006 ICI-treated patients) preclude precise effect estimation. Absolute excess risk 0.40% is consistent with ICI therapy in other cancer types but confidence interval cannot be calculated.
