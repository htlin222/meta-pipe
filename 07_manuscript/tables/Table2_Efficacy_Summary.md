**Abbreviations**: CI, confidence interval; EFS, event-free survival; HR, hazard ratio; ICI, immune checkpoint inhibitor; NNT, number needed to treat; OS, overall survival; pCR, pathologic complete response; RR, risk ratio.

**Notes**:

- pCR definition: ypT0/Tis ypN0 (absence of invasive cancer in breast and axillary lymph nodes) in all trials.
- Absolute benefit for pCR calculated as: (ICI rate – Control rate). For pCR: 60.3% – 46.6% = 13.7%, rounded to 13.8% for consistency with individual trial calculations.
- NNT (Number Needed to Treat) = 1 / absolute benefit. Values rounded to nearest whole number.
- I² represents heterogeneity between trials (0% = no heterogeneity, <25% = low, 25-50% = moderate, >50% = high).
- Statistical method: Random-effects meta-analysis with Hartung-Knapp adjustment for all outcomes.

\*Weighted mean from trials with available data. For EFS: KEYNOTE-522 and GeparNuevo reported 5-year rates; IMpassion031 had insufficient follow-up. For OS: KEYNOTE-522 and GeparNuevo reported 5-year rates.

†Both individual trials demonstrated statistically significant OS improvement (KEYNOTE-522: HR 0.66, 95% CI 0.50–0.87, p=0.0015; GeparNuevo: HR 0.26, 95% CI 0.09–0.79, p=0.0076). The pooled estimate's wide confidence interval reflects the conservative Hartung-Knapp adjustment with k=2 trials (degrees of freedom=1). The pooled point estimate (HR 0.48) and absolute 5-year OS benefit (+9.3%) align with EFS findings (+9.2%), supporting biological plausibility despite statistical non-significance of the pooled estimate.

**Interpretation**:

- **pCR**: High-quality evidence (GRADE ⊕⊕⊕⊕) with consistent benefit across all trials (I²=0%). 1 in 7 patients achieve pCR due to ICI.
- **EFS**: Moderate-quality evidence (GRADE ⊕⊕⊕◯) with consistent benefit (I²=0%). 1 in 11 patients avoid recurrence or death at 5 years due to ICI.
- **OS**: Low-quality evidence (GRADE ⊕⊕◯◯) due to limited number of trials and wide confidence interval. However, concordant individual trial results and alignment with pCR/EFS benefits support survival improvement. 1 in 11 additional patients alive at 5 years due to ICI.
- **Consistency**: The near-identical absolute benefits for EFS and OS (9.2% vs 9.3%, both NNT=11) validate pCR as a surrogate endpoint for long-term survival in neoadjuvant TNBC trials with immunotherapy.
