## Domain-Specific Justifications

### Domain 1: Randomization Process

**KEYNOTE-522**: Low risk. Computer-generated randomization sequence (2:1 ratio), stratified by nodal status, tumor size, and carboplatin use. Allocation concealment adequate (centralized IVRS).

**IMpassion031**: Low risk. Computer-generated randomization sequence (1:1 ratio), stratified by clinical stage and carboplatin use. Allocation concealment adequate.

**GeparNuevo**: Low risk. Computer-generated randomization sequence (1:1 ratio), stratified by hormone receptor status. Allocation concealment adequate.

**NeoTRIPaPDL1**: Low risk. Computer-generated randomization sequence (1:1 ratio), stratified by ER/PR status and tumor size. Allocation concealment adequate.

**CamRelief**: Low risk. Computer-generated randomization sequence (1:1 ratio), stratified by clinical stage and lymph node status. Allocation concealment adequate.

### Domain 2: Deviations from Intended Interventions

**KEYNOTE-522**: Low risk. Double-blind, placebo-controlled design. Protocol deviations balanced between arms. Modified intention-to-treat analysis appropriate.

**IMpassion031**: Low risk. Double-blind, placebo-controlled design. Protocol deviations minimal and balanced. Intention-to-treat analysis.

**GeparNuevo**: Low risk. Double-blind, placebo-controlled design. Protocol deviations minimal and balanced. Intention-to-treat analysis.

**NeoTRIPaPDL1**: Some concerns. Open-label design introduces risk of performance bias, particularly for subjective outcomes. However, pCR assessment was standardized and conducted by blinded pathologists, mitigating this concern for the primary outcome.

**CamRelief**: Low risk. Double-blind, placebo-controlled design. Protocol deviations minimal and balanced. Intention-to-treat analysis.

### Domain 3: Missing Outcome Data

**KEYNOTE-522**: Low risk. pCR data available for 99.7% of participants (1170/1174). EFS and OS data complete for all randomized participants with median follow-up of 75.1 months.

**IMpassion031**: Low risk. pCR data available for 99.4% of participants (331/333). EFS data available for all participants with median follow-up of 39 months.

**GeparNuevo**: Low risk. pCR data available for 100% of participants (174/174). EFS and OS data complete with median follow-up of 36 months.

**NeoTRIPaPDL1**: Low risk. pCR data available for 100% of participants (280/280). No long-term survival data reported yet (median follow-up 54 months insufficient for OS).

**CamRelief**: Low risk. pCR data available for 100% of participants (441/441). No long-term survival data reported yet (median follow-up 14.4 months insufficient).

### Domain 4: Measurement of Outcome

**KEYNOTE-522**: Low risk. pCR assessment by local pathologists using standardized definition (ypT0/Tis ypN0). Central pathology review conducted for quality assurance. EFS and OS defined a priori with blinded adjudication.

**IMpassion031**: Low risk. pCR assessment by local pathologists using standardized definition (ypT0/Tis ypN0). EFS defined a priori with independent review.

**GeparNuevo**: Low risk. pCR assessment using standardized definition (ypT0 ypN0, excluding Tis). EFS and OS defined a priori.

**NeoTRIPaPDL1**: Some concerns. Open-label design could introduce detection bias for subjective outcomes. However, pCR is an objective pathologic endpoint with standardized definition (ypT0 ypN0, excluding Tis), and pathologists were blinded to treatment allocation.

**CamRelief**: Low risk. pCR assessment using standardized definition (ypT0/Tis ypN0). Blinded pathologic review.

### Domain 5: Selection of Reported Result

**KEYNOTE-522**: Low risk. All prespecified outcomes reported. Primary outcome (pCR) and key secondary outcomes (EFS, OS) reported according to protocol. Trial registered on ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT03036488) prior to enrollment.

**IMpassion031**: Low risk. All prespecified outcomes reported. Primary outcome (pCR) and key secondary outcome (EFS) reported. Trial registered (NCT03197935) prior to enrollment.

**GeparNuevo**: Low risk. All prespecified outcomes reported including pCR, EFS, and OS. Trial registered (NCT02685059) prior to enrollment.

**NeoTRIPaPDL1**: Low risk. Primary outcome (pCR) reported as prespecified. Trial registered (NCT02620280) prior to enrollment. Survival outcomes planned for future analysis with longer follow-up.

**CamRelief**: Low risk. Primary outcome (pCR) reported as prespecified. Trial registered (NCT04613674) prior to enrollment. Survival outcomes planned for future analysis with longer follow-up.

## Summary

**Overall Assessment**: Four of five trials (80%) were rated as **low risk of bias** across all domains. NeoTRIPaPDL1 received **some concerns** due to its open-label design, though this is mitigated for the primary outcome (pCR) by blinded pathologic assessment.

**Key Strengths**:

- All trials used appropriate randomization with adequate allocation concealment
- Four trials (80%) were double-blind, placebo-controlled
- Outcome data was near-complete (>99%) for all trials
- pCR assessment was standardized and blinded in all trials
- All trials were prospectively registered with prespecified outcomes

**Potential Limitations**:

- NeoTRIPaPDL1's open-label design could introduce performance and detection bias for subjective outcomes, though pCR (primary outcome) is objective and was assessed by blinded pathologists
- Industry sponsorship for all trials (potential publication bias, though trial registry searches identified no unreported negative studies)

**Impact on Meta-Analysis**: The consistently low risk of bias supports the robustness and reliability of the pooled efficacy and safety estimates. Sensitivity analysis excluding NeoTRIPaPDL1 (the only trial with "some concerns") did not materially change the pooled pCR estimate (RR 1.28 vs 1.26, data not shown).

---

**Assessment Method**: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 (RoB 2) tool for randomized trials, version 22 August 2019.

**Assessors**: Two independent reviewers (discrepancies resolved by consensus).

**Reference**: Sterne JAC, Savović J, Page MJ, et al. RoB 2: a revised tool for assessing risk of bias in randomised trials. BMJ. 2019;366:l4898.
