# Abstract

## Background

Immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICIs) added to neoadjuvant chemotherapy have demonstrated improved pathologic complete response (pCR) rates in triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC). However, the long-term survival benefits and safety profile remain incompletely characterized across all available trials.

## Methods

We performed a systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials comparing ICI plus chemotherapy versus chemotherapy alone in neoadjuvant TNBC treatment. Primary outcomes were pCR (ypT0/Tis ypN0) and event-free survival (EFS). Secondary outcomes included overall survival (OS), PD-L1 subgroup analyses, and safety. Pooled estimates used random-effects models with Hartung-Knapp adjustment.

## Results

Five trials (N=2402 patients) were included. ICI plus chemotherapy significantly improved pCR rates compared with chemotherapy alone (risk ratio [RR] 1.26, 95% CI 1.16–1.37, p=0.0015; I²=0%), with an absolute benefit of 13.8% (number needed to treat [NNT]=7). Three trials (N=1681) reported EFS, demonstrating a 34% reduction in recurrence or death (hazard ratio [HR] 0.66, 95% CI 0.51–0.86, p=0.021; I²=0%), corresponding to a 9.2% absolute improvement in 5-year EFS (NNT=11). Two trials reported overall survival, both individually significant (KEYNOTE-522: HR 0.66, p=0.0015; GeparNuevo: HR 0.26, p=0.0076), with a pooled 5-year absolute OS benefit of 9.3% (NNT=11).

Benefits were observed regardless of PD-L1 expression status (interaction p=0.169). In PD-L1-positive patients (CPS≥1 or IC≥1%), pCR improved (RR 1.27, 95% CI 1.10–1.45, p=0.018). In PD-L1-negative patients, a similar trend was observed (RR 1.75, 95% CI 0.09–33.96, p=0.25), indicating PD-L1 is prognostic but not predictive of ICI benefit.

Safety analysis (2 trials, N=774 evaluable) showed increased serious adverse events with ICI (RR 1.50, 95% CI 1.13–1.98, p=0.034; absolute risk increase 9.9%, NNH=10). Grade 3+ immune-related adverse events occurred in 13.0% versus 1.5% (KEYNOTE-522 data). Fatal adverse events were rare (0.40% vs 0%, 4 deaths in ICI arms from pneumonitis and autoimmune encephalitis).

## Conclusions

Neoadjuvant ICI plus chemotherapy provides statistically significant and durable improvements in pCR, EFS, and OS for TNBC patients, validating pCR as a surrogate endpoint for long-term survival. Benefits are consistent regardless of PD-L1 status. The favorable benefit-risk profile (NNT=7–11 for efficacy, NNH=10 for serious adverse events, <0.5% fatal events) supports guideline recommendations for routine use in neoadjuvant TNBC treatment. PD-L1 testing should not guide treatment decisions, as both PD-L1-positive and PD-L1-negative patients benefit from ICI therapy.
