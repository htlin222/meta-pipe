## 18. Participants/Population

### Inclusion Criteria

**Disease**:
- Histologically confirmed invasive breast cancer
- Triple-negative phenotype: ER-negative, PR-negative, HER2-negative
- ER/PR negative: <1% nuclear staining by immunohistochemistry (IHC)
- HER2 negative: IHC 0-1+, or IHC 2+ with negative FISH/ISH (HER2/CEP17 ratio <2.0)

**Stage**:
- Early-stage: Clinical stage I-III
- Locally advanced: T2-4, N0-3, M0
- Resectable disease at diagnosis
- Candidate for curative-intent surgery

**Treatment Setting**:
- Neoadjuvant (preoperative) systemic therapy
- No prior systemic therapy for current breast cancer

**Patient Characteristics**:
- Adults: Age ≥18 years
- ECOG performance status 0-2 (or equivalent)
- Adequate organ function for chemotherapy and immunotherapy

### Exclusion Criteria

**Disease**:
- Metastatic disease (stage IV, M1)
- Inflammatory breast cancer (unless explicitly eligible in trial)
- Bilateral breast cancer (unless both are TNBC)
- Recurrent breast cancer
- HER2-positive or hormone receptor-positive disease

**Treatment**:
- Prior systemic therapy (chemotherapy, targeted therapy, immunotherapy) for current breast cancer
- Prior chest wall radiotherapy
- Contraindications to immunotherapy or chemotherapy

**Other**:
- Active autoimmune disease requiring systemic therapy
- Concurrent malignancy (except adequately treated basal cell carcinoma or cervical carcinoma in situ)
- Pregnancy or lactation

## 19. Intervention(s), Exposure(s)

### Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor plus Neoadjuvant Chemotherapy

**Immunotherapy Agents**:
- Pembrolizumab (target: PD-1)
- Atezolizumab (target: PD-L1)
- Durvalumab (target: PD-L1)
- Nivolumab (target: PD-1)
- Camrelizumab (target: PD-1)

**Timing**: Given concurrently with neoadjuvant chemotherapy
**Duration**: Typically 4-8 cycles during neoadjuvant phase

**Chemotherapy Regimens**:
- Anthracycline-taxane based regimens
- Platinum-taxane based regimens
- Combination regimens (platinum-taxane followed by anthracycline-cyclophosphamide)

**Adjuvant Phase**: May continue immunotherapy after surgery per trial protocol

## 22. Context

This systematic review addresses a critical question in the management of early-stage triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC), the most aggressive breast cancer subtype. While Villacampa et al. (2024) recently published a meta-analysis of neoadjuvant immune checkpoint inhibitors across all breast cancer subtypes, our review provides:

1. **TNBC-exclusive focus**: Deep dive into triple-negative subtype specifically
2. **Updated evidence**: Inclusion of 2025 data (e.g., CamRelief trial)
3. **TNBC-specific subgroup analyses**: PD-L1 expression, tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes, androgen receptor status
4. **Long-term survival emphasis**: Latest overall survival data from landmark trials (e.g., KEYNOTE-522 with 75-month follow-up)

## 23. Primary Outcome(s)

**1. Pathologic Complete Response (pCR)**

**Definition**: ypT0/Tis ypN0

**Description**: No residual invasive cancer in breast and axillary lymph nodes

**Alternative Definitions**:
- ypT0 ypN0 (no residual invasive or in situ cancer)
- ypT0/Tis (breast only, regardless of nodal status)

**Timing**: At time of definitive surgery

**Measurement**: Histopathological assessment by local pathologist

**Effect Measure**: Risk Ratio (RR) with 95% CI

---

## 24. Secondary Outcomes

**1. Event-Free Survival (EFS)**
Definition: Time from randomization to first occurrence of: disease progression preventing surgery, locoregional recurrence, distant recurrence, second primary cancer, or death from any cause
Timing: From randomization to event or last follow-up
Effect Measure: Hazard Ratio (HR) with 95% CI

**2. Overall Survival (OS)**
Definition: Time from randomization to death from any cause
Timing: From randomization to death or last follow-up
Effect Measure: Hazard Ratio (HR) with 95% CI

**3. Disease-Free Survival (DFS)**
Definition: Time from surgery to disease recurrence or death from any cause
Timing: From surgery to event or last follow-up
Effect Measure: Hazard Ratio (HR) with 95% CI


**4. Grade 3-4 Adverse Events**
Definition: Treatment-related adverse events grade 3 or higher per CTCAE
Effect Measure: Risk Ratio (RR) with 95% CI

**5. Immune-Related Adverse Events (irAEs)**
Definition: Adverse events of special interest related to immune checkpoint inhibition
Effect Measure: Risk Ratio (RR) with 95% CI

## 25. Data Extraction (Selection and Coding)

### Selection Process
- **Dual independent screening**: Two reviewers will independently screen titles/abstracts and full texts
- **Screening tool**: Rayyan (https://www.rayyan.ai/)
- **Conflict resolution**: Disagreements resolved by consensus; third reviewer consulted if no consensus
- **Documentation**: Reasons for exclusion at full-text stage will be documented

### Data Extraction Process
- **Dual independent extraction**: Two reviewers will independently extract data from included studies
- **Extraction tool**: Standardized data extraction form (data-dictionary.md)
- **Pilot extraction**: Extraction form piloted on 2 studies before full extraction
- **LLM-assistance**: Claude CLI may be used to assist with initial extraction, followed by manual verification
- **Verification**: 20% of studies will undergo dual extraction for quality assurance

### Data Items
**Study characteristics**: Author, year, trial name, phase, sample size, country, funding
**Population**: Age, stage, receptor status, PD-L1 status, TILs
**Intervention**: ICI type, dose, schedule, chemotherapy regimen, adjuvant continuation
**Outcomes**: pCR definition and rate, HR for EFS/DFS/OS with 95% CI, median follow-up, adverse events
**Risk of bias items**: Randomization method, blinding, missing data, selective reporting

## 27. Strategy for Data Synthesis

### Meta-Analysis Methods

**Effect Measures**:
- Risk Ratio (RR) with 95% for binary outcomes (pCR)
- Hazard Ratio (HR) with 95% for time-to-event outcomes (EFS, DFS, OS)

**Meta-Analysis Model**: Random-effects model (DerSimonian-Laird)
**Rationale**: Expect moderate heterogeneity due to different ICIs and chemo regimens

**Heterogeneity Assessment**:
- I² statistic
- Cochran's Q test (p-value)
- τ² (between-study variance)

**Interpretation**:
- I² 0-40%: Low heterogeneity
- I² 40-60%: Moderate heterogeneity
- I² 60-100%: Substantial heterogeneity

**Software**: R (meta, metafor packages)

## 28. Analysis of Subgroups or Subsets

**1. PD-L1 Expression Status**
Categories: PD-L1 positive (CPS ≥1%), PD-L1 negative (CPS <1%)
Rationale: PD-L1 is a predictive biomarker for ICI response

**2. Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor Type**
Categories: Pembrolizumab (PD-1 inhibitor), Atezolizumab (PD-L1 inhibitor), Durvalumab (PD-L1 inhibitor), Camrelizumab (PD-1 inhibitor), Other ICIs
Rationale: Different ICIs may have different efficacy profiles

**3. ICI Target**
Categories: PD-1 inhibitors, PD-L1 inhibitors
Rationale: PD-1 vs PD-L1 targeting may differ in efficacy

**4. Chemotherapy Backbone**
Categories: Anthracycline-taxane based, Platinum-taxane based, Anthracycline-free regimens
Rationale: Chemotherapy regimen may influence ICI synergy

**5. Disease Stage**
Categories: Early stage (T1-2, N0-1), Locally advanced (T3-4 or N2-3)
Rationale: Stage may affect treatment response

**6. Adjuvant ICI Continuation**
Categories: ICI continued into adjuvant phase, ICI stopped after neoadjuvant phase
Rationale: Adjuvant ICI may contribute to survival benefit
