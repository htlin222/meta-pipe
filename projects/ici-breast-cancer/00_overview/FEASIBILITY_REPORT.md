## Decision Rationale (Hour 4)

### Why GO?

1. **Sufficient high-quality studies**: 5-8 Phase II-III RCTs identified, well-powered (KEYNOTE-522 alone has n=1174)

2. **Consistent outcome reporting**: pCR universally reported with clear effect sizes, survival data available in mature trials

3. **Clinical meaningfulness**: All studies compare same intervention (ICI + chemo vs chemo) in same population (early TNBC)

4. **Excellent data accessibility**: Clear reporting in tables/figures, estimated 3 hours total extraction time

5. **Active field**: 87% published in last 5 years, results will be current and clinically relevant

6. **Established methodology**: Villacampa et al. 2024 proves feasibility and journal interest

### Addressing the Competitor Meta-Analysis

**Villacampa et al. 2024** published similar analysis. **Our differentiation strategy**:

1. **TNBC-specific focus**: Narrow exclusively to triple-negative subtype if Villacampa included all breast cancers
2. **Include 2025 data**: CamRelief and any other newly published studies
3. **More detailed subgroup analyses**:
   - By PD-L1 expression levels (CPS <1, 1-9, 10-19, ≥20)
   - By ICI type and target (pembrolizumab vs atezolizumab vs camrelizumab; PD-1 vs PD-L1)
   - By chemotherapy backbone (platinum-first vs taxane-first vs anthracycline-first)
   - By adjuvant ICI continuation (yes vs no)
   - By disease stage (T1-2 vs T3-4)
4. **Long-term survival emphasis**: Include latest OS data (KEYNOTE-522 2024 OS results)
5. **Individual patient data (IPD)**: Consider contacting trial authors for patient-level subgroup data

**Conclusion**: Competitor analysis is not a barrier; it validates feasibility and provides methodological precedent. Our TNBC-specific focus with updated data and detailed subgroups will provide meaningful new insights.

## Expected Challenges and Mitigation Strategies

| Challenge                                 | Severity | Impact                            | Mitigation Strategy                                             |
| ----------------------------------------- | -------- | --------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Moderate ICI heterogeneity**            | ⚠️ Minor | I² ~30-60%                        | Use random-effects model, subgroup by ICI type, meta-regression |
| **Immature survival data**                | ⚠️ Minor | OS not available in all studies   | Primary outcome: pCR (all studies); Secondary: EFS/OS (subset)  |
| **Competitor meta-analysis**              | ⚠️ Minor | Need to justify novelty           | TNBC-specific, include 2025 data, detailed subgroups, IPD       |
| **Multiple publications from same trial** | ⚠️ Minor | Risk of duplicate data            | Use latest publication, cite all, careful data extraction       |
| **Adjuvant ICI continuation varies**      | ⚠️ Minor | Effect may include adjuvant phase | Extract continuation data, sensitivity analysis                 |
| **Publication bias**                      | ⚠️ Minor | Industry-sponsored trials         | Funnel plot, Egger's test, search trial registries              |

**All challenges are manageable. No fatal flaws identified.**

## Anticipated Meta-Analysis Characteristics

### Primary Analysis

- **Outcome**: pCR (pathologic complete response, ypT0/Tis ypN0)
- **Effect measure**: Risk Ratio (RR) with 95% CI
- **Model**: Random-effects (DerSimonian-Laird or REML)
- **Studies**: 5-8 RCTs
- **Participants**: ~2500-3000 total (estimated)

### Secondary Analyses

1. **Event-Free Survival**
   - Effect measure: HR with 95% CI
   - Studies: Subset with mature data (3-5 RCTs)

2. **Overall Survival**
   - Effect measure: HR with 95% CI
   - Studies: Subset with mature data (2-4 RCTs)

### Subgroup Analyses (Planned)

1. By PD-L1 status (positive ≥1% vs negative <1%)
2. By ICI type (pembrolizumab vs atezolizumab vs camrelizumab)
3. By ICI target (PD-1 vs PD-L1)
4. By chemotherapy backbone (anthracycline-based vs platinum-based)
5. By disease stage (T1-2 vs T3-4)
6. By adjuvant ICI continuation (yes vs no)

### Sensitivity Analyses (Planned)

1. Exclude phase II trials (only phase III)
2. Exclude trials with high risk of bias
3. Fixed-effects model (compare to random-effects)
4. Leave-one-out analysis (especially KEYNOTE-522 due to large size)
