### 3. Outcome Reporting: 2/2 points ✅

**Evidence**:

**pCR reporting** (3/3 pilot studies):

- KEYNOTE-522: 64.8% vs 51.2%, clear event counts
- IMpassion031: 57.6% vs 41.1%, clear event counts
- CamRelief: Expected to report (primary outcome)

**Survival reporting**:

- KEYNOTE-522: HR 0.63 (EFS), HR 0.72 (OS) with 95% CIs
- Other studies: Variable maturity, but pCR universally reported

**Assessment**: All studies report quantitative effect sizes with sufficient detail for meta-analysis

---

### 4. Clinical Homogeneity: 1/2 points ⚠️

**Similarities** (promotes homogeneity):

- ✅ All target PD-1/PD-L1 pathway
- ✅ All in neoadjuvant setting
- ✅ All compare ICI + chemo vs chemo alone
- ✅ All in early/locally advanced TNBC
- ✅ All use platinum-taxane-anthracycline backbones

**Differences** (introduces heterogeneity):

- ⚠️ Different ICI drugs: pembrolizumab (PD-1), atezolizumab (PD-L1), camrelizumab (PD-1)
- ⚠️ Different chemotherapy sequences
- ⚠️ Adjuvant ICI continuation varies
- ⚠️ Different geographic populations (global vs China)

**Heterogeneity management strategy**:

- Primary analysis: Pool all ICIs (same mechanism of action)
- Subgroup analysis: By ICI type (pembrolizumab vs atezolizumab vs others)
- Sensitivity analysis: By chemotherapy backbone
- Meta-regression: Adjuvant continuation as covariate

**Assessment**: Moderate heterogeneity expected (I² likely 30-60%), but clinically meaningful to pool with appropriate subgroup analyses

---

### 5. Population Similarity: 2/2 points ✅

**Evidence**:

- **Disease**: All triple-negative breast cancer (ER-, PR-, HER2-)
- **Stage**: All early or locally advanced (not metastatic)
- **Setting**: All neoadjuvant (preoperative)
- **Intent**: All curative intent with planned surgery

**Minor variations**:

- Some trials may include T1-2 N1-2, others T2-3 N0-1
- Age ranges likely similar (adults ≥18 years)
- Performance status likely ECOG 0-1 across all trials

**Assessment**: Highly homogeneous population, minimal concern for population-level heterogeneity

### 8. Precedent: 2/2 points ✅

**Existing meta-analysis**:

- Published in major journal
- Covers similar topic (neoadjuvant ICIs in early breast cancer)

**Implications**:

**Positive** (Feasibility):

- ✅ Proves the question is answerable
- ✅ Demonstrates methodology is established
- ✅ Shows there's journal interest in this topic
- ✅ Can use as reference for missed studies

**Negative** (Competition):

- ⚠️ Similar analysis already published (2024)
- ⚠️ Need to differentiate our analysis

**Differentiation strategy**:

1. **TNBC-specific focus**: If Villacampa included all breast cancer subtypes, narrow to TNBC only
2. **Include 2025 data**: CamRelief and any other 2025 studies
3. **More detailed subgroup analyses**:
   - By PD-L1 status (CPS <1 vs ≥1, ≥10, ≥20)
   - By ICI type and dose
   - By chemotherapy backbone
   - By adjuvant continuation
4. **Individual patient data (IPD)**: Consider contacting authors for patient-level subgroup data
5. **Long-term survival focus**: If Villacampa focused on pCR, we emphasize EFS/OS with longer follow-up

**Assessment**: Strong precedent proves feasibility, competition is manageable with focused differentiation

## Expected Challenges

1. **Heterogeneity (I²)**: Expect moderate I² (30-60%) due to different ICIs and chemo regimens
   - **Mitigation**: Use random-effects model, subgroup analyses, meta-regression

2. **Publication bias**: Industry-sponsored trials may have selective reporting
   - **Mitigation**: Funnel plots, Egger's test, search clinical trial registries for unpublished data

3. **Immature survival data**: OS may not be available in all trials
   - **Mitigation**: Primary outcome is pCR (all studies report), survival as secondary

4. **Differentiation from Villacampa 2024**: Need to justify novelty
   - **Mitigation**: TNBC-specific focus, include newest data, more detailed subgroups
