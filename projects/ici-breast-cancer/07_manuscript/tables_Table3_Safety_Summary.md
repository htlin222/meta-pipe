**Abbreviations**: CI, confidence interval; irAE, immune-related adverse event; NNH, number needed to harm; RR, risk ratio.

**Notes**:

- Serious Adverse Events: Meta-analyzed from IMpassion031 and CamRelief. Events requiring hospitalization, life-threatening events, or resulting in significant disability.
- Grade 3+ irAE: KEYNOTE-522 only. Immune-related adverse events of Grade 3 or higher per CTCAE v4.0. Common irAEs included thyroid dysfunction (47% any grade), pneumonitis, colitis, and hepatitis.
- Treatment Discontinuation: KEYNOTE-522 only. Discontinuation of study treatment (ICI or placebo) due to adverse events during neoadjuvant phase. Most discontinuations were due to immune-related toxicities and did not negate pCR benefits already achieved.
- Fatal Adverse Events: KEYNOTE-522 (2 deaths: pneumonitis, autoimmune encephalitis) and CamRelief (2 deaths, detailed attribution not reported). All deaths occurred in ICI arms.
- NNH (Number Needed to Harm) = 1 / absolute harm. Represents number of patients needed to treat for one additional patient to experience the adverse outcome.
- Absolute harm calculated as: (ICI rate – Control rate).

\*KEYNOTE-522 data only; other trials did not report these specific safety outcomes in detail.

†RR estimated from event rates; confidence intervals not calculated for single-trial data.

**Comparison with Real-World Data**:

- Grade 3+ irAE rates in this meta-analysis (13.0% in KEYNOTE-522) are consistent with real-world cohorts:
  - UK cohort (N=257): 13.6% Grade 3+ irAEs
  - Dutch cohort (N=156): 12% Grade 3+ irAEs
- Fatal AE rate (0.40%) is within expected range for ICI therapy across cancer types (0.3–0.8%)

**Clinical Management**:

- **Grade 2 irAEs**: Consider ICI interruption and corticosteroids
- **Grade 3–4 irAEs**: Permanent ICI discontinuation and high-dose immunosuppression (prednisone 1–2 mg/kg/day)
- **Endocrinopathies**: Require lifelong hormone replacement (levothyroxine for hypothyroidism, hydrocortisone for adrenal insufficiency) but are generally well-tolerated
- **Monitoring**: Baseline and serial thyroid function, liver enzymes, creatinine recommended

**Benefit-Risk Assessment**:
For every 10 patients treated with ICI plus chemotherapy versus chemotherapy alone:

- **Benefits**: ~1.5 achieve additional pCR (NNT=7), ~1 remains alive and disease-free at 5 years (NNT=11)
- **Harms**: ~1 experiences serious AE (NNH=10), ~1 experiences Grade 3+ irAE (NNH=9), ~0.04 experiences fatal AE (NNH=250)
- **Conclusion**: Curative potential substantially outweighs manageable and typically reversible toxicity

**Interpretation**:

- Most immune-related adverse events are reversible with established management protocols
- Fatal events are rare (4 deaths among 1006 ICI-treated patients across 2 trials)
- Treatment discontinuation does not eliminate benefits already achieved (patients achieving pCR before discontinuation still have improved EFS)
- The benefit-risk profile strongly favors ICI plus chemotherapy in neoadjuvant TNBC treatment
