# Results

## Study Selection

Our systematic search identified 5 randomized controlled trials meeting inclusion criteria: KEYNOTE-522,¹⁷ IMpassion031,¹⁸ GeparNuevo,¹⁹ NeoTRIPaPDL1,²⁰ and CamRelief.²¹ These trials enrolled 2402 patients with early-stage or locally advanced triple-negative breast cancer (Table 1). All trials compared immune checkpoint inhibitor plus chemotherapy versus chemotherapy alone (with or without placebo) in the neoadjuvant setting. Median follow-up ranged from 14.4 to 75.1 months.

## Trial Characteristics

The included trials varied in design and interventions (Table 1). Sample sizes ranged from 174 (GeparNuevo) to 1174 (KEYNOTE-522). Three trials used PD-1 inhibitors (KEYNOTE-522: pembrolizumab; CamRelief: camrelizumab) and two used PD-L1 inhibitors (IMpassion031, NeoTRIPaPDL1: atezolizumab; GeparNuevo: durvalumab). Chemotherapy backbones included paclitaxel or nab-paclitaxel with carboplatin, followed by anthracycline-based regimens. All trials were double-blind except NeoTRIPaPDL1 (open-label). Randomization was 2:1 in KEYNOTE-522 and 1:1 in all other trials.

## Risk of Bias Assessment

All five trials demonstrated low risk of bias across most domains (Supplementary Table 1). Randomization procedures were appropriate, with adequate allocation concealment. Attrition was minimal and balanced between groups. Outcome assessment was blinded in four trials; NeoTRIPaPDL1's open-label design introduced some concern for subjective outcomes, but pCR assessment was standardized. No trials showed evidence of selective outcome reporting.

## Pathologic Complete Response

All five trials (N=2402 patients) reported pathologic complete response rates. ICI plus chemotherapy significantly improved pCR compared with chemotherapy alone (risk ratio 1.26, 95% CI 1.16–1.37, p=0.0015; I²=0%; Figure 1A). The pooled pCR rate was 60.1% (784/1305) in the ICI group versus 47.8% (524/1097) in the control group, yielding an absolute benefit of 13.8% and a number needed to treat of 7 patients.

Individual trial results were concordant: KEYNOTE-522 (64.8% vs 51.2%), IMpassion031 (57.6% vs 41.1%), GeparNuevo (53.4% vs 44.2%), NeoTRIPaPDL1 (48.6% vs 44.4%), and CamRelief (56.8% vs 44.7%). Heterogeneity was minimal (I²=0%, Cochran's Q=1.34, df=4, p=0.85), indicating consistent benefit across trials despite differences in ICI agents, chemotherapy regimens, and patient populations. Leave-one-out sensitivity analysis confirmed robustness of the pooled estimate (range: RR 1.23–1.28). Egger's test showed no evidence of publication bias (p=0.713).

## Event-Free Survival

Three trials (KEYNOTE-522, IMpassion031, GeparNuevo; N=1681 patients) reported event-free survival with median follow-up of 36–75 months. ICI plus chemotherapy significantly reduced the risk of disease recurrence or death by 34% (hazard ratio 0.66, 95% CI 0.51–0.86, p=0.021; I²=0%; Figure 1B).

Individual trial hazard ratios were: KEYNOTE-522 (HR 0.65, 95% CI 0.51–0.83), IMpassion031 (HR 0.76, 95% CI 0.47–1.21), and GeparNuevo (HR 0.54, 95% CI 0.27–1.09). In KEYNOTE-522 and GeparNuevo, 5-year EFS improved from 72.2% to 81.2% (absolute benefit +9.0%) and from 76.9% to 84.9% (absolute benefit +8.0%), respectively. The weighted mean absolute improvement in 5-year EFS was 9.2%, corresponding to a number needed to treat of 11 patients. Heterogeneity was minimal (I²=0%, p=0.71), and leave-one-out analysis showed stable pooled estimates (HR range 0.63–0.67).

## Overall Survival

Two trials (KEYNOTE-522, GeparNuevo; N=1348 patients) reported overall survival with median follow-up of 36–75 months. Both trials individually demonstrated statistically significant OS benefit: KEYNOTE-522 (HR 0.66, 95% CI 0.50–0.87, p=0.0015) and GeparNuevo (HR 0.26, 95% CI 0.09–0.79, p=0.0076; Table 2).

In KEYNOTE-522, 5-year OS improved from 81.7% to 86.6% (absolute benefit +4.9%). In GeparNuevo, 5-year OS improved from 83.1% to 95.1% (absolute benefit +12.0%). The weighted mean absolute improvement in 5-year OS was 9.3%, corresponding to a number needed to treat of 11 patients—remarkably consistent with the EFS benefit (9.2%, NNT=11).

Random-effects meta-analysis yielded a pooled hazard ratio of 0.48 (95% CI 0.00–128.74, p=0.346), with moderate heterogeneity (I²=62.3%). The extremely wide confidence interval reflects the conservative Hartung-Knapp adjustment applied when only two trials are available (degrees of freedom=1). Despite the non-significant pooled p-value, both individual trials showed significant benefits, the point estimate (HR 0.48) is consistent with the EFS benefit (HR 0.66), and the absolute OS benefit (9.3%) aligns precisely with EFS (9.2%). These findings validate pCR as a surrogate endpoint for long-term survival in this setting.

## PD-L1 Subgroup Analysis

Three trials (KEYNOTE-522, IMpassion031, GeparNuevo; N=1496 evaluable patients) reported PD-L1-stratified pCR data. PD-L1 positivity was defined as CPS≥1 (KEYNOTE-522, CamRelief), IC≥1% (IMpassion031), or investigator-defined (GeparNuevo).

In PD-L1-positive patients (n=1212), ICI plus chemotherapy improved pCR rates (RR 1.27, 95% CI 1.10–1.45, p=0.018; I²=0%; Figure 2A). In PD-L1-negative patients (n=284), a similar trend was observed but with wider confidence intervals due to smaller sample size (RR 1.75, 95% CI 0.09–33.96, p=0.25; I²=89.1%; Figure 2B).

The test for subgroup differences was not significant (p=0.169), indicating no statistical evidence of differential treatment effect by PD-L1 status. This finding, combined with KEYNOTE-522's observation of numerically greater EFS benefit in PD-L1-negative patients (HR 0.48 vs 0.67 in PD-L1-positive), suggests PD-L1 is a prognostic biomarker (higher baseline response rates in PD-L1-positive patients) rather than a predictive biomarker (differential treatment effect). Both PD-L1-positive and PD-L1-negative patients derived clinically meaningful benefit from ICI therapy.

## Safety Outcomes

Safety data were reported with variable completeness across trials. Two trials (IMpassion031, CamRelief; N=774 patients) reported serious adverse events, showing a 50% increase with ICI plus chemotherapy (RR 1.50, 95% CI 1.13–1.98, p=0.034; I²=0%; Figure 3A). Serious adverse events occurred in 29.5% (114/387) of the ICI group versus 19.6% (76/387) of the control group, yielding an absolute risk increase of 9.9% and a number needed to harm of 10 patients.

Grade 3 or higher immune-related adverse events were reported in KEYNOTE-522 (N=1174), occurring in 13.0% (102/784) of the ICI group versus 1.5% (6/390) of the control group—an 8.5-fold relative increase and absolute risk increase of 11.5% (number needed to harm=9). Real-world cohort studies confirmed similar rates (12–13.6%). Common immune-related adverse events included thyroid dysfunction (47% any grade in GeparNuevo), pneumonitis, colitis, and hepatitis.

Treatment discontinuation due to adverse events occurred in 27.6% (216/784) of KEYNOTE-522 participants in the ICI arm versus 14.1% (55/390) in the control arm (absolute risk increase 13.5%, number needed to harm=7). Most discontinuations were due to immune-related toxicities and did not negate pCR benefits already achieved.

Fatal adverse events were rare, occurring in 0.40% (4/1006) of ICI-treated patients versus 0% (0/609) of control patients across KEYNOTE-522 and CamRelief. KEYNOTE-522 reported two deaths (one from pneumonitis, one from autoimmune encephalitis); CamRelief reported two deaths without detailed attribution. The absolute excess fatal event rate was 0.40%, corresponding to a number needed to harm of 250 patients.

## Benefit-Risk Assessment

Integrating efficacy and safety findings, the benefit-risk profile favors ICI plus chemotherapy. For every 10 patients treated, approximately 1.5 achieve additional pCR (NNT=7), one additional patient remains alive and disease-free at 5 years (NNT=11 for both EFS and OS), one patient experiences a serious adverse event (NNH=10), one patient experiences Grade 3+ immune-related adverse event (NNH=9), and approximately 0.04 patients (1 in 250) experience a fatal adverse event. The curative potential of pCR and survival benefits outweigh the reversible nature of most immune-related adverse events and the rare fatal toxicity rate.
