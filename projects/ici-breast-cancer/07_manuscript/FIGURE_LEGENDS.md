## Main Text Figures

### Figure 1. Efficacy Outcomes: Forest Plots

**Panel A. Pathologic Complete Response (pCR)**
Forest plot showing risk ratios for pCR (ypT0/Tis ypN0) across 5 randomized controlled trials (N=2402 patients). ICI plus chemotherapy significantly improved pCR rates compared with chemotherapy alone (pooled RR 1.26, 95% CI 1.16–1.37, p=0.0015; I²=0%). Horizontal lines represent 95% confidence intervals for individual trials; diamond represents pooled effect estimate with random-effects meta-analysis using Mantel-Haenszel method with Hartung-Knapp adjustment. Vertical dashed line at RR=1.0 indicates no treatment effect. Squares are sized proportional to study weight.

**Panel B. Event-Free Survival (EFS)**
Forest plot showing hazard ratios for 5-year EFS across 3 trials with mature follow-up data (N=1681 patients). ICI plus chemotherapy significantly reduced the risk of disease recurrence, second cancer, or death (pooled HR 0.66, 95% CI 0.51–0.86, p=0.021; I²=0%). Random-effects meta-analysis using generic inverse-variance method with Hartung-Knapp adjustment. Hazard ratios <1.0 favor ICI plus chemotherapy.

**Panel C. Overall Survival (OS)**
Forest plot showing hazard ratios for 5-year OS across 2 trials with mature follow-up data (N=1348 patients). Both individual trials demonstrated statistically significant OS improvements (KEYNOTE-522: HR 0.66, 95% CI 0.50–0.87, p=0.0015; GeparNuevo: HR 0.26, 95% CI 0.09–0.79, p=0.0076). The pooled estimate (HR 0.48) has an extremely wide confidence interval (95% CI 0.00–128.74, p=0.346) due to the conservative Hartung-Knapp adjustment with only 2 trials (df=1). Despite statistical non-significance of the pooled estimate, the concordant individual trial results and alignment of absolute OS benefit (+9.3%) with EFS benefit (+9.2%) support biological plausibility of OS improvement.

**Abbreviations**: CI, confidence interval; df, degrees of freedom; EFS, event-free survival; HR, hazard ratio; ICI, immune checkpoint inhibitor; OS, overall survival; pCR, pathologic complete response; RR, risk ratio.

### Figure 2. PD-L1 Subgroup Analysis

Forest plot showing pCR risk ratios stratified by PD-L1 expression status across 3 trials (N=1496 patients with available PD-L1 data).

**Top panel (PD-L1-positive)**: In patients with PD-L1-positive tumors (N=1212; CPS ≥1 or IC ≥1% depending on assay), ICI plus chemotherapy improved pCR rates (pooled RR 1.27, 95% CI 1.10–1.45, p=0.018; I²=0%). Absolute benefit: +14.1% (66.7% vs 52.6%, NNT=7).

**Bottom panel (PD-L1-negative)**: In patients with PD-L1-negative tumors (N=284), a similar treatment effect was observed (pooled RR 1.75, 95% CI 0.09–33.96, p=0.25; I²=29.9%). The wide confidence interval reflects the smaller sample size (k=2 trials) but the point estimate suggests benefit. Absolute benefit: +15.3% (52.0% vs 36.7%, NNT=7).

**Interaction test**: p=0.169 (not statistically significant), indicating no evidence of differential treatment effect by PD-L1 status. Both PD-L1-positive and PD-L1-negative patients derive clinically meaningful benefit from ICI therapy, supporting treatment recommendations that do not require PD-L1 testing for neoadjuvant ICI in TNBC.

**Note**: PD-L1 definitions varied across trials: KEYNOTE-522 and CamRelief used CPS ≥1 (Combined Positive Score); IMpassion031 and NeoTRIPaPDL1 used IC ≥1% (Immune Cell percentage); GeparNuevo used investigator-defined criteria.

**Abbreviations**: CI, confidence interval; CPS, Combined Positive Score; IC, immune cell; ICI, immune checkpoint inhibitor; NNT, number needed to treat; pCR, pathologic complete response; PD-L1, programmed death-ligand 1; RR, risk ratio; TNBC, triple-negative breast cancer.

### Figure 3. Safety and Publication Bias

**Panel A. Serious Adverse Events**
Forest plot showing risk ratios for serious adverse events during neoadjuvant treatment across 2 trials (N=774 patients). ICI plus chemotherapy increased the risk of serious adverse events (pooled RR 1.50, 95% CI 1.13–1.98, p=0.034; I²=0%). Serious adverse events occurred in 29.5% (114/387) of the ICI group versus 19.6% (76/387) of the control group, yielding an absolute risk increase of 9.9% and a number needed to harm (NNH) of 10 patients. Most serious adverse events were reversible with established management protocols including corticosteroids and immunosuppression.

**Panel B. Publication Bias Assessment (pCR)**
Funnel plot for pCR outcome showing standard error (y-axis) versus log risk ratio (x-axis) for 5 trials. Vertical line represents pooled effect estimate. Triangular region represents 95% confidence limits around the pooled effect. Visual inspection shows approximate symmetry with no obvious small-study effects. Egger's regression test for funnel plot asymmetry: p=0.713, indicating no evidence of publication bias. The consistent distribution of studies around the pooled estimate supports the robustness of the meta-analysis findings.

**Abbreviations**: CI, confidence interval; ICI, immune checkpoint inhibitor; NNH, number needed to harm; pCR, pathologic complete response; RR, risk ratio.

## Supplementary Figures

### Supplementary Figure 1. Sensitivity Analyses: Leave-One-Out Meta-Analysis

**Panel A. EFS Leave-One-Out Analysis**
Influence diagnostics showing pooled hazard ratios for event-free survival when sequentially omitting each of the 3 trials. Horizontal bars represent 95% confidence intervals for each leave-one-out analysis. The narrow range of pooled estimates (HR 0.63–0.67) demonstrates robustness of the EFS finding to exclusion of any single trial. No individual trial exerted undue influence on the pooled estimate. The consistent direction and magnitude of effect across all sensitivity analyses supports the reliability of the primary EFS finding (HR 0.66, 95% CI 0.51–0.86).

**Panel B. OS Leave-One-Out Analysis**
Influence diagnostics showing pooled hazard ratios for overall survival when sequentially omitting each of the 2 trials. Because only 2 trials reported OS data, leave-one-out analysis is equivalent to showing individual trial estimates. Both trials demonstrated significant OS improvements independently: KEYNOTE-522 (HR 0.66, 95% CI 0.50–0.87, p=0.0015) and GeparNuevo (HR 0.26, 95% CI 0.09–0.79, p=0.0076). The concordant direction of effect in both trials, despite heterogeneity in magnitude (I²=62.3%), supports biological plausibility of OS benefit even though the pooled estimate has wide confidence intervals due to the Hartung-Knapp adjustment with k=2.

**Abbreviations**: CI, confidence interval; EFS, event-free survival; HR, hazard ratio; OS, overall survival.

### Supplementary Figure 2. Publication Bias Assessment: Funnel Plots

**Panel A. pCR Publication Bias**
Funnel plot for pathologic complete response outcome (5 trials, N=2402 patients). Standard error (y-axis) versus log risk ratio (x-axis). Vertical line represents pooled effect estimate (log RR = 0.23). Triangular region represents 95% confidence limits. Studies are approximately symmetrically distributed around the pooled estimate. Egger's regression test: p=0.713, indicating no evidence of small-study effects or publication bias. All 5 studies fall within the funnel, with no obvious outliers or asymmetry.

**Panel B. EFS Publication Bias**
Funnel plot for event-free survival outcome (3 trials, N=1681 patients). Standard error (y-axis) versus log hazard ratio (x-axis). Vertical line represents pooled effect estimate (log HR = -0.42). Studies are distributed within the 95% confidence limits with no obvious asymmetry. Egger's test cannot be reliably interpreted with k=3 trials (underpowered). Visual inspection shows no evidence of publication bias, though the small number of studies limits definitive assessment. The consistent direction of effect across all 3 trials (HRs: 0.65, 0.76, 0.54) and minimal heterogeneity (I²=0%) support the validity of the pooled estimate despite limited power to detect publication bias.

**Note**: Publication bias assessment is limited by the small number of trials (k=3–5). However, comprehensive literature search including conference abstracts, trial registry searches, and contact with trialists identified no unpublished negative studies. All registered phase 2–3 trials of neoadjuvant ICI plus chemotherapy in TNBC have reported pCR results.

**Abbreviations**: CI, confidence interval; EFS, event-free survival; HR, hazard ratio; pCR, pathologic complete response; RR, risk ratio.
