## Research Question
Which frontline chemoimmunotherapy regimen achieves the best progression-free survival in previously untreated diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL)? An updated network meta-analysis of Phase III randomized controlled trials with subgroup analyses by cell-of-origin, IPI risk, double-expressor status, and age.

## PICO Elements

**Population**: Adults (≥18 years) with previously untreated DLBCL (de novo, any stage)
**Intervention**: Novel frontline regimens tested in Phase III RCTs:
  - Pola-R-CHP (polatuzumab vedotin + R-CHP)
  - R-CHOP + ibrutinib
  - R-CHOP + lenalidomide
  - R-CHOP + bortezomib
  - G-CHOP (obinutuzumab-CHOP)
  - DA-EPOCH-R (dose-adjusted EPOCH + rituximab)
**Comparator**: R-CHOP (network common node)
**Outcomes**:
  - Primary: Progression-free survival (PFS) — HR with 95% CI
  - Secondary: Overall survival (OS) — HR with 95% CI
  - Secondary: Complete response rate (CR) — RR or OR
  - Secondary: Grade ≥3 adverse events — RR
  - Secondary: Treatment-related mortality

## Study Design
Phase III randomized controlled trials only
- Exclude Phase II, single-arm, and retrospective studies
- Include both published papers and conference abstracts with sufficient data (HR + CI)

## Planned Subgroup Analyses (Key Innovation)

1. **Cell-of-origin (COO)**: ABC/non-GCB vs GCB
   - Rationale: POLARIX 5y shows ABC benefit (OS HR dramatic); PHOENIX designed for ABC; ROBUST enrolled ABC only
2. **IPI risk score**: IPI 0-2 (low) vs IPI 3-5 (high)
   - Rationale: POLARIX shows differential benefit by IPI; "ceiling effect" in low-risk
3. **Double-expressor lymphoma (DEL)**: MYC+BCL2 co-expression
   - Rationale: POLARIX HR 0.64 in DEL — strongest signal
4. **Age**: ≤60 vs >60 (or >70 if data available)
   - Rationale: POLARIX shows benefit in >70; safety profiles differ

## Potential Challenges
1. Star-shaped network limits indirect comparisons between novel agents
2. Subgroup data (COO, DEL) not reported in all trials
3. Different PFS definitions across trials (IRC vs investigator)
4. ROBUST enrolled ABC-only — population heterogeneity
5. Variable follow-up durations (2-5 years)

## Mitigation Strategies
1. Use consistency model; report SUCRA rankings with uncertainty
2. Perform subgroup NMA only with ≥3 trials reporting; note missing data
3. Sensitivity analysis by PFS definition
4. ROBUST handled as ABC-subgroup data; integrate with ABC subgroups from other trials
5. Use most mature data available; note follow-up in evidence table

## TP53 Mutation Gap (Future Direction)

- Insufficient data for NMA subgroup analysis (few trials report TP53 outcomes)
- Flag in Discussion as unmet need: venetoclax combinations, bispecific antibodies, CAR-T
