Literature Map: The Cognitive Cost of Thinking Hard
Why sustained effortful cognition produces discomfort — a cognitive neuroscience framing

TYPE: Scoping Review (NOT meta-analysis — field not ready for quantitative synthesis)

RESEARCH QUESTION:
Why does sustained cognitive effort produce a discomfort signal, and why does that signal
sometimes manifest as a headache-like sensation — even in healthy individuals with no
headache disorder?

PICO-S:
  P: Healthy adults (no current headache diagnosis, no migraine in past 3 months)
  I: Sustained cognitive task >= 30 min (N-back, Stroop, working memory, attentional control, simulated workday)
  C: Rest or low-demand control condition
  O: Somatic discomfort, effort discounting, fatigue ratings, head discomfort, pupillometry, fMRI/MRS metabolic markers
  S: Any study design (RCT, within-subjects, observational, theoretical, review)

FIVE LITERATURE LAYERS:
  L1. Why effort is aversive (opportunity cost model, effort paradox, EVC)
  L2. Neural metabolic cost of sustained cognitive work (glutamate accumulation, glycogen depletion, lPFC fatigue)
  L3. Effort as aversive signal — the phenomenology (dACC as effort meter, aversive motivation)
  L4. Predictive processing / allostatic framing (free energy principle, interoceptive prediction error)
  L5. Pain/headache literature bridging (stress-hyperalgesia, pericranial tenderness, TTH mechanisms)
