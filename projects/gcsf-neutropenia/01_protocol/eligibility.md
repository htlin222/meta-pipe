# Eligibility Criteria

**Project**: gcsf-neutropenia
**Date**: 2026-03-24

---

## Inclusion Criteria

### Population
- Adult patients (age >= 18 years)
- Diagnosed with solid tumors or hematologic malignancies
- Receiving myelosuppressive chemotherapy (any regimen)
- No restriction on cancer type, stage, or line of therapy
- No restriction on geographic region or healthcare setting

### Intervention
- Prophylactic G-CSF administration using any of the following:
  - **Filgrastim** (daily G-CSF; e.g., Neupogen, biosimilars such as Zarxio, Nivestym)
  - **Pegfilgrastim** (pegylated G-CSF; e.g., Neulasta, biosimilars such as Udenyca, Fulphila)
  - **Lipegfilgrastim** (glyco-pegylated G-CSF; e.g., Lonquex)
- Primary prophylaxis (initiated in cycle 1) or secondary prophylaxis (initiated after a prior episode of FN or severe neutropenia)
- Any dose or schedule consistent with labeled use or clinical guidelines (e.g., ASCO, ESMO, NCCN)

### Comparator
- Placebo (matching injection)
- No G-CSF prophylaxis (standard supportive care)
- Note: Studies comparing one G-CSF formulation against another are eligible for inclusion in the network meta-analysis

### Outcomes
- Studies must report at least one of the following:
  - Febrile neutropenia incidence (primary)
  - Any secondary outcome listed in pico.yaml

### Study Design
- Randomized controlled trials (RCTs) only
- Parallel-group or crossover design
- Published in peer-reviewed journals or as conference abstracts with sufficient data
- No language restriction (non-English studies will be translated)
- No minimum sample size requirement

---

## Exclusion Criteria

### Population
- Pediatric patients (age < 18 years)
- Patients receiving G-CSF for stem cell mobilization
- Patients receiving G-CSF for treatment (not prophylaxis) of established neutropenia
- Patients with congenital or chronic neutropenia (not chemotherapy-induced)
- Patients receiving bone marrow/stem cell transplant conditioning regimens

### Intervention
- Therapeutic (rather than prophylactic) G-CSF use
- GM-CSF (sargramostim) as the intervention (different mechanism)
- Other myeloid growth factors not classified as G-CSF

### Study Design
- Observational studies (cohort, case-control, cross-sectional)
- Single-arm trials without a comparator
- Case reports and case series
- Narrative reviews, editorials, letters, and commentaries
- Systematic reviews and meta-analyses (will be hand-searched for primary studies)
- Animal and in vitro studies
- Phase I dose-finding studies focused solely on pharmacokinetics
- Studies with no extractable outcome data

### Other
- Duplicate publications (the most complete report will be used)
- Studies retracted from publication

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Febrile neutropenia (FN) | ANC < 0.5 x 10^9/L (or ANC < 1.0 x 10^9/L with predicted decline to < 0.5) AND single oral temperature >= 38.3 C or >= 38.0 C sustained over 1 hour |
| Severe neutropenia | ANC < 0.5 x 10^9/L (grade 3-4 per CTCAE) |
| Primary prophylaxis | G-CSF initiated in the first chemotherapy cycle before any FN episode |
| Secondary prophylaxis | G-CSF initiated in subsequent cycles after a prior FN episode |
| Myelosuppressive chemotherapy | Any cytotoxic regimen associated with >= 10% baseline risk of FN |

---

## Notes

- If studies include mixed populations (e.g., adults and adolescents >= 16 years), they will be included if the majority (> 80%) of participants are >= 18 years or if adult-specific data can be extracted.
- Studies comparing different G-CSF formulations head-to-head (e.g., pegfilgrastim vs filgrastim) will be included in the network meta-analysis but not in the pairwise comparison against placebo.
